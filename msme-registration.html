Frequently Asked Questions

MSME / Udyam Registration FAQs

Clear answers to the most common questions about MSME and Udyam Registration in India.

Who can register under MSME / Udyam?
Any manufacturing or service business with investment and turnover within prescribed limits can register. Micro enterprises must have investment below ₹1 Crore and turnover below ₹5 Crore. Small enterprises: below ₹10 Crore investment and ₹50 Crore turnover. Medium enterprises: below ₹50 Crore investment and ₹250 Crore turnover. Both manufacturing and service businesses are eligible.
Is Udyam registration free?
Yes, Udyam registration on the official government portal (udyamregistration.gov.in) is completely free of charge. There is no government fee whatsoever. Eleven IX charges a nominal professional fee for assisting you with accurate filing.
Is the old Udyog Aadhaar valid?
No. The government has officially replaced Udyog Aadhaar with the new Udyam Registration system effective July 2020. You must migrate your old Udyog Aadhaar to Udyam Registration to continue receiving MSME benefits and subsidies from the government.
How long is MSME registration valid?
Udyam Registration is permanent with no expiry date — you do not need to renew it annually. However, you must update your registration if your investment or annual turnover changes category. If your turnover crosses the Medium enterprise limit, you will no longer qualify as an MSME.
What is the difference between MSME and Udyam?
Udyam is simply the new name for MSME registration, introduced in 2020 by the Government of India under the Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises. Both terms refer to exactly the same registration. The Udyam system replaced the older Udyog Aadhaar system and offers a more digital, paperless process.
