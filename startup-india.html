Frequently Asked Questions

Startup India DPIIT FAQs

Everything you need to know about DPIIT Startup India Recognition in India.

Who qualifies for Startup India registration?
Any entity — Private Limited Company, LLP or Partnership Firm — that is less than 10 years old from the date of incorporation, has an annual turnover below ₹100 Crore in any financial year, and is working on an innovative product, process or service with high scalability potential qualifies for DPIIT recognition under Startup India.
Is Startup India registration free?
Yes, applying for DPIIT recognition on the official Startup India portal (startupindia.gov.in) is completely free of charge. There is no government fee for the recognition application. Eleven IX charges a professional fee for preparing and managing your application.
What tax benefits do I get?
DPIIT-recognised startups get 3 consecutive years of complete income tax exemption out of the first 10 years of incorporation (Section 80-IAC), subject to IMB certification. They also get full Angel Tax exemption (Section 56) — meaning investment received from Indian resident investors is not taxed as income. Additionally, there is an 80% rebate on patent filing fees and a waiver of trademark registration fees for DPIIT-recognised startups.
Can a service company register as a startup?
Yes, absolutely. Any innovative product, process or service company is eligible to apply for Startup India DPIIT recognition. The business does not have to be a technology company — it only needs to demonstrate innovation, scalability and a significant potential for wealth or employment creation.
How long does DPIIT recognition take?
Typically 2–3 weeks after submitting the application with all required documents on the Startup India portal. DPIIT reviews your business description and innovation claim, and upon approval, issues the recognition certificate digitally. Eleven IX will track your application and resolve any queries raised during review.
