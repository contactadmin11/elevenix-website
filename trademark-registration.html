Why Register Your Trademark

4 Powerful Reasons to Protect Your Brand

A registered trademark is one of the most valuable business assets you can own. Here's what it does for you.

⚡

Exclusive Rights

You get the sole legal right to use your brand name and logo across India. No other business can use an identical or confusingly similar mark in your registered class.

⚖️

Legal Protection

Sue infringers in a court of law. A registered trademark lets you send cease-and-desist notices and claim damages against anyone who copies your brand.

💎

Brand Value

The ™ and ® symbols instantly increase customer trust and brand credibility. Registered trademarks can be licensed, franchised or sold as business assets.

🔄

10-Year Protection

Your trademark is valid for 10 years from the date of registration. It can be renewed indefinitely every 10 years, giving you lifelong brand ownership.

Frequently Asked Questions

Trademark Registration FAQs

Everything you need to know about trademark registration in India.

What can be trademarked?
Brand names, logos, slogans, product names, and even sounds and colors can be registered as trademarks in India. Anything that distinctively identifies your business and its goods or services qualifies for trademark protection.
How long does trademark registration take?
Initial filing takes 24 hours. Full registration typically takes 12–18 months as it goes through examination by the Trademark Registry and a mandatory 4-month publication window in the Trademarks Journal.
Can I use ™ before registration?
Yes, you can use the ™ symbol immediately after filing your trademark application. However, you can only use the ® (registered) symbol after the trademark is fully registered by IP India.
What are trademark classes?
IP India has 45 trademark classes: Classes 1–34 cover goods (e.g., food, clothing, electronics) and Classes 35–45 cover services (e.g., advertising, IT services, legal services). You must file in the correct class(es) relevant to your business to ensure full protection.
What if someone copies my trademark?
A registered trademark gives you the legal right to send cease-and-desist notices and file infringement suits in court. You can also claim damages and seek an injunction to stop the infringing party from using your brand.
