Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about income tax return filing in India.

Who must file an ITR? +

Anyone earning above ₹2.5 lakhs annually (₹3 lakhs for senior citizens aged 60–79 years, and ₹5 lakhs for super senior citizens aged 80 or above) must file an Income Tax Return. Filing is also mandatory for those with foreign assets, those claiming refunds, or those with business/professional income, regardless of the amount.

What is the last date for ITR filing? +

The last date for filing ITR without audit is July 31 of the assessment year for most individual taxpayers. For businesses requiring audit, the due date is October 31. Do not miss the deadline — late fees of up to ₹5,000 apply.

Can I file ITR for previous years? +

Yes, you can file a belated return (with late fee) up to December 31 of the assessment year. Updated returns (ITR-U) can also be filed up to 2 years from the end of the relevant financial year under Section 139(8A). Contact us for guidance on your specific situation.

What is Form 16? +

Form 16 is a TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) certificate issued by your employer at the end of each financial year. It shows your total salary paid, allowances, perquisites, and the amount of income tax deducted by your employer. It is the primary document needed for ITR filing by salaried individuals.

What happens if I don’t file ITR? +

Not filing ITR can result in a late filing fee of up to ₹5,000 under Section 234F, interest on any unpaid tax under Sections 234A and 234B, notices from the Income Tax Department, and difficulty in obtaining loans, credit cards, or visas as ITR is often required as proof of income. File on time to avoid all these issues.
